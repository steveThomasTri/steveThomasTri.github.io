Part 6: Thinking Outside the Pocket

Our first model doesn't represent the problem accurately. What should we have considered and how do we factor that in to a future model.

Although in our first model we were able to get 70.21% accuracy, the problem is that this model doesn't accurately represent what the coaches are looking for. Because NFL teams need to fill different positions and different positions require different attributes, the first model is not a fair representation of what is happening in the real world. It is not enough to think about the measurements of a player, you also have to think about the intangibles of that individual. You have to put on many hats as a data scientist, such as in this case, a coach, a scout, a GM, an owner, etc. How do you measure leadership? How do you evaluate how well they interview? What is their work ethic on and off the field? More importantly, how would one quantify intangibles? For some of these intangibles, they are not available to the public and thus it may be impossible to measure like interviews, white board test, Wonderlic test. One word about the Wonderlic test is that some of these scores are leaked to the public and we cannot verify the validity of these scores. Here is a list of factors that were considered and not considered. Keep in mind that the items not considered may not be an exhaustive list.

Factors that I have considered:
  • Prospect's height and weight
  • Prospect's measurements in the 6 events
  • Prospect's college stats
  • If any awards were received (Heisman, all-American)
  • Prospect's position
Factors that I have NOT considered:
  • Prospect's arm length and hand length
  • Prospect's college and conference
  • Prospect's interviews, white board test, and Wonderlic test
  • How tough was their conference
  • Their grit (leadership, work ethic, toughness, football intelligence)
  • Different teams needs to fill certain positions
  • Any tapes that scouts looked at to assess their playing ability
  • And much more...

There are other factors that were not considered and I have a list of two articles that outline these intangibles. The first article is from The Ringer one year ago. This article outlines how scouts evaluate qualities like poise, confidence, grit, etc. The second article is a starting point on how the Bleacher Report breaks down certain positions. The article starts off with what scouts look for in a safety. There are more links in other positions if you are interested. There are other articles out there, but these are the two main ones to get a general understanding on other factors than just raw numbers.

From suggestions that were given to me. I have decided that instead of one model, I would have a model for each group. With each model, determine what combine events are important in an attempt to reduce noise and make the models more of like what to look for in those positions. So starting from our 8 original features, let's see if there are some features that we would like to add, or delete.
